Dear Editors,

I am pleased to submit the Software Tool Article "FairQueue: a transparent, fairness-aware simulator for prioritising NHS elective-care pressure using public data" for consideration on F1000Research.

FairQueue is an open-source Python tool that turns public, aggregated NHS and ONS data into a transparent, explainable and fairness-aware prioritisation of elective-care pressure at the level of NHS provider × specialty × month. Unlike existing descriptive dashboards, it combines waiting-list pressure, fairness risk (over-representation of deprivation, ethnicity, age and sex groups among the longest waits) and operational pressure (diagnostics, A&E, beds, theatres and cancelled operations) using visible weighted formulas, and lets users compare five prioritisation strategies. It adds an automated data-preparation engine for cleaning and joining uploaded datasets, and a machine-learning model with an explainable-AI layer and a built-in bias audit.

The tool prioritises areas of the health system rather than individual patients, and uses no patient-identifiable data, keeping it within the bounds of safe public-data health-services research. The source code is openly available under the MIT licence, an archived version is deposited on Zenodo, and all underlying data are public. I believe the article fits F1000Research's aims around open, reproducible research software.
